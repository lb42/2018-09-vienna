Description and goals

This hands-on workshop provides a total of 7-1/2 hours of instruction in using eXtensible Markup Language (XML) and the Text Encoding Initiative (TEI) guidelines for the encoding and description of medieval manuscript materials. The instructors are:

  • David J. Birnbaum, a medieval Slavic philologist who teaches digital humanities at the University of Pittsburgh (djbpitt@gmail.com, http://www.obdurodon.org)
  • Lou Burnard, formerly assistant director at Oxford University Computing Services, and one of the original editors of the Text Encoding Initiative. (lou.burnard@gmail.com, http://users.ox.ac.uk/~lou)

The workshop is part of the Textual heritage and information technologies (El’Manuscript-2018) conference, Vienna and Krems, Austria, 2018-09-14–2018-09-18.

Before the workshop

  • This is a hands-on workshop. You will need a laptop running Windows, MacOS, or Linux. (ChromeOS, iOS, and other netbook or tablet operating systems are not supported.)
  • Download <oXgyen/> XML editor from http://www.oxygenxml.com and install it on your laptop. There are other <oXygen/> products on the same page; you want XML Editor. We recommend the version that is bundled with Java, even if you already have Java installed (the Java that is downloaded with <oXygen/> will not interfere with other Java installations on your machine). You need a license to operate <oXygen/>; you can request a free, fully-functional thirty-day license at the download site.
  • Read: An even gentler introduction to XML.
  • If you have time, also read What is the Text Encoding Initiative? (also available in French)
  • In the hands-on portion of the workshop we will mostly work with Church Slavonic manuscript materials. If you prefer, you are welcome instead to bring your own materials (plain-text manuscript descriptions or transcriptions).

Manuscript description

Many on-line manuscript catalogues provide convenient search interfaces. Here are a few that go beyond that, and might be considered models for how digital manuscript descriptions can support not just convenient access, but also new types of analysis and visualization. All provide access to the underlying raw XML.

  • The Repertorium of Old Bulgarian literature and letters provides analytical descriptions of over one hundred early Slavic manuscripts, with tools for searching by repository information and by textual content. The prototype plectogram of Physiologus texts illustrates a graphic representation of textual correspondences within the corpus.
  • The goal of e-codices (Virtual manuscript library of Switzerland) is to provide access to all medieval and selected early modern manuscripts of Switzerland via a virtual library. At the moment, the virtual library contains 2048 manuscripts from 84 different collections. In addition to the general web site, a free iOS app is available in the Apple store. Note the integration of overview, facsimile, and formatted descriptions, and the provision of the raw XML.
  • handrit.is is an analytical catalog of Icelandic manuscripts currently held in several institutions. It provides sophisticated browsing and search facilities and has an integrated personography, which provides access to biographical information about persons mentioned in the descriptions.
